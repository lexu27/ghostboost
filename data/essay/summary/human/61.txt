The essay delves into the effects of COVID-19 on students in Saudi Arabia, with a specific focus on anxiety. By utilizing Zung's self-rating anxiety questionnaire through online surveys, the research examines anxiety levels concerning various factors such as gender, age, and academic year. Valuable insights are gleaned regarding the impact of the pandemic on university students who have not contracted the virus directly. However, the study's scope is limited to one university, potentially constraining its applicability to a broader population. It highlights not only the direct consequences of COVID-19 but also the indirect repercussions, including heightened anxiety and stress among individuals. Ultimately, the article is positioned to serve as a vital resource for those seeking data on anxiety-related trends, contributing significantly to the existing knowledge in this area.
