Blockchain technology, introduced in 2008 by Satoshi Nakamoto, serves as a secure method for recording and storing information. Its primary purpose is to prevent any retroactive changes to data, thus upholding the integrity of information. Typically linked to cryptocurrencies and decentralized deposit guarantee systems, blockchain has the capacity to transform intellectual property on a global scale. With its innovative features and robust security measures, blockchain stands out as a groundbreaking technology that could pave the way for significant advancements in various industries.
