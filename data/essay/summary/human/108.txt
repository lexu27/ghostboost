Black women around the world encounter obstacles when seeking high-quality healthcare services during pregnancy and childbirth, which not only affects their own health but also that of their offspring. It is imperative for all women to have access to quality care to promote the well-being of both the mother and child. Implementing systemic changes in healthcare systems can play a pivotal role in improving the overall health outcomes of black women, increasing their access to superior healthcare services, and ultimately enhancing maternal health results. Ensuring that black women have access to top-notch healthcare is fundamental for securing the future well-being of not just their children, but society as a whole.
