Arguably, profit and cash flow differ because profit cost considered an amount that a business entity is left with after settling all the business firm ’ s expenses. Contrary, cash flow is an aggregate net flow in the form of cash both into the business and proscribed of the business operations. Therefore, cash flow is considered as attainable operational balance and conclusive monetary coverage (Sitompul & Khadijah, 2020 ). While cash flow existence can occur without necessarily a firm gaining a profit, the existence of profit is only estimated after a positive balance of trade. It is believable that the firm ’ s cash flow and profit embody essential aspects. For instance, understanding both will allow the company coach to guess the firm ’ s operational capabilities while ensuring that the business can stand on information technology feet in terms of paying for its expenses. Additionally, while profit point positive returns to the company, cash flow indicates positive operations that contribute to desirable business decisions.
