Diasporas, those scattered communities residing beyond their ancestral homelands, wield a profound influence on the global economy. Their impact has surged in recent times, challenging the traditional belief that the economic prowess of ethnic groups remains localized. This essay delves into the escalating significance of diasporas in the global economy, underscoring how their economic prowess now rivals, and in certain instances, surpasses that of the ethnic groups inhabiting their immediate surroundings. It scrutinizes the factors propelling this transition, delves into the ways in which diasporas enrich global trade, investment, and innovation, and deliberates on the advantages and hurdles entwined with their economic assimilation.
