The landscape of education has been dramatically reshaped by the COVID-19 pandemic, ushering in a new era of online learning that has revolutionized traditional teaching methods. Initially daunting for students like myself, who were accustomed to the brick-and-mortar classroom environment, the transition to virtual education has revealed its silver lining upon closer examination. The allure of flexibility in setting one's own learning tempo and delving into subjects beyond the conventional curriculum has emerged as a prominent advantage. Nonetheless, it is essential to acknowledge the hurdles that accompany this educational shift, particularly the dearth of in-person interactions with peers and instructors that can impede the holistic learning journey.
