Introduction

Ecological significance has long been attributed to the Anchorage Management Area, particularly for its diverse and abundant fish populations. However, in recent times, a noticeable decline in fish availability has emerged, presenting significant challenges to the productivity and appeal of local fisheries. This essay seeks to delve into the historical context, existing management approaches, and performance data spanning from 2016 to 2018, in order to illuminate the multifaceted repercussions of dwindling fish availability on the local fisheries within the Anchorage Management Area.
