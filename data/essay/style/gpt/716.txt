Modern warfare has undergone a notable transformation in its strategic methods, thanks to the emergence of unmanned aerial vehicles, more commonly referred to as drones. The deployment of drones in military operations has revolutionized tactical approaches, enabling nations to exert influence and neutralize threats from distant locations. Nevertheless, this technological advancement gives rise to a plethora of ethical dilemmas concerning the potential use of deadly force, accountability, and decision-making procedures. In the forthcoming discussion, we shall delve into the ethical ramifications of employing drones in warfare, with a specific focus on the decision-making processes influenced by ethical egoism, existentialism, and hedonism, meticulously scrutinizing their respective implications.
