Connie's susceptibility to Arnold Friend's manipulative tactics in Joyce Carol Oates' poignant short story, "Where Are You Going, Where Have You Been?", is deeply intertwined with her complex family background. The strained relationships within her family, particularly with her mother and father, create a fertile ground for Arnold's deceptive maneuvers. Connie's constant yearning for validation from external sources, stemming from her family's inability to provide emotional nourishment, sets the stage for her vulnerability to Arnold's cunning schemes.
