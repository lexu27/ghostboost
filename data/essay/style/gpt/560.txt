Introduction

The role of adequate dental care in maintaining overall health and quality of life, especially for seniors, cannot be overstated. Yet, the absence of dental care coverage for seniors and the escalating costs of treatments present formidable obstacles. This essay seeks to explore the current landscape of Medicare, its exclusion of dental services, the financial burdens associated with dental procedures, and the repercussions of insufficient dental care. Additionally, it will delve into proposed solutions, including President Biden's initiative to expand Medicare, in order to address these pressing issues.
