The 19th Amendment, which granted women the right to vote, was a significant milestone in American history as the 20th century commenced. Various perspectives on its importance concerning women’s rights and societal change were shaped by a range of factors, including racism and race-based biases. Suffragettes put forth diverse arguments regarding the amendment's impact on women’s emancipation and societal dynamics, whereas some Tennessee women opposed the amendment due to ingrained racism. This essay will delve into these perspectives, illuminating the intricate relationship between gender and race during this era.
