The Flint water crisis, a tragic event in American history with lasting repercussions, remains a poignant reminder of governmental failure. This essay delves into the crisis through a Marxist lens, dissecting the class conflict between the working class and the bourgeoisie in Flint. An examination of Flint's socioeconomic landscape sheds light on the neglect that led to this disaster, offering insights into its root causes. By unraveling these layers, we pave a path towards potential solutions to avert such calamities in the future.
