Emily Dickinson, a prominent figure in American literary history, stands out as a significant poet of the 19th century. Renowned for her distinctive style and enigmatic themes, Dickinson delves deeply into the complexities of human existence, capturing the essence of hope, freedom, and the individual's interaction with society within her work "Hope Is the Thing with Feathers." This particular poem gains heightened relevance when viewed against the backdrop of the Civil War, a tumultuous period that deeply fractured American society. By delving into the historical context of the Civil War, as well as delving into Dickinson's personal background, her use of phonetic elements, and the varying tones within her poetry, we can attain a profound insight into its social implications.
