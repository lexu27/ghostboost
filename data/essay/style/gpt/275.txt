Introduction:
Mahatma Gandhi, revered as the Father of the Indian nation, exemplified not only political leadership but also a profound embodiment of moral values and principles. His philosophy, recognized as Satyagraha, emphasized the profound impact of non-violence and truth, shaping India's quest for independence and resonating in global movements advocating justice and peace. Gandhi's legacy endures through his teachings on inter-religious harmony and social justice, influencing contemporary India significantly. Despite his revered status, there are concerns that Gandhi's philosophy has been distorted in modern times. This essay will delve into the moral principles, inter-religious relations, and enduring legacy of Mahatma Gandhi, while examining the alleged corruption of his philosophy in present-day India.
