South Korea has seen a surge in popularity on the global stage, thanks to its vibrant popular culture, encompassing K-pop music and Korean dramas, collectively known as Hallyu. This essay will delve into the impact of these cultural phenomena on the promotion of rural and urban areas in South Korea. Referencing Youjeong Oh's work, "Korean Popular Culture and the Selling of Place," this piece will scrutinize the tactics utilized by the nation to draw in tourists through K-pop, K-drama, K-food, plastic surgery, and tourism. Furthermore, it will probe the link between urban development and Hallyu, exploring how Korean popular culture influences the commercialization of rural and urban landscapes in South Korea.
