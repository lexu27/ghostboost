Bovine Spongiform Encephalopathy, also known as "mad cow disease," became a notable public health issue in the late 20th century. Initially affecting cattle with severe brain damage, strict controls were put in place within the beef industry. However, subsequent findings indicated a potential transmission of BSE to humans through contaminated meat ingestion. This essay explores the potential human transmission of BSE, its public health implications, and the measures taken to safeguard meat consumption.
