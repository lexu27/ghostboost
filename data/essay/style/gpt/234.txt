Ebola virus disease, a severe and often fatal illness caused by the Ebola virus of the Filoviridae family, has sparked devastating outbreaks primarily in Sub-Saharan Africa. This essay delves into the pathophysiology of EVD, its community impact, and the remediation efforts against the outbreak. It delves into transmission routes, preparedness shortcomings, public health literacy, and cultural practices, providing a holistic view of the challenges faced by affected communities and the strategies employed to combat them.
