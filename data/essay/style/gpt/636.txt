Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems have sparked a revolution in healthcare by converting traditional paper-based medical records into digitized, easily accessible formats. The integration of EHR systems into nursing practice has garnered considerable attention for their ability to enhance patient care, streamline workflows, foster interprofessional collaboration, and boost patient satisfaction. This essay delves into the significance of incorporating EHR systems into nursing practice and puts forth a policy for utilizing EHR in drug administration, medication monitoring, treatment adherence, and substance abuse screening. Additionally, this essay delves into the challenges and constraints of existing EHR systems, emphasizing the imperative need for optimization to bolster efficient workflows, interprofessional care, and patient satisfaction.
