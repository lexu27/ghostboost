Forecasting serves as a fundamental element in decision-making processes across diverse fields, striving to anticipate forthcoming events by analyzing historical data and pertinent factors. Embedded in this structure is the pivotal role of judgmental forecasting, which amalgamates the subjective viewpoints of experts and decision-makers. This essay endeavors to delve into the realm of judgmental forecasting, exploring its concept, applications, efficacy in decision-making, and scrutinizing its merits and demerits through illustrative instances.
