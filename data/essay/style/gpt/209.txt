Body image has long captivated the attention of researchers and society alike. Yet, prevailing literature has predominantly fixated on the body image dilemmas faced by women, overshadowing the narratives of men and the intricate web connecting gender, masculinity, and body image apprehensions. This essay ventures to scrutinize the link between masculinity and the pursuit of muscularity, while also probing into the potential ties between femininity and concerns about body shape. Furthermore, it aspires to discern whether disparities in body image concerns exist between the genders.
