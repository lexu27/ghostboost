Prejudice, a bias deeply ingrained against specific groups or individuals, has endured across the annals of human history. Its origins often link back to the impacts of religion, media, and societal identities, crucial players in nurturing and preserving prejudice. This essay delves into the intricate interconnections among religion, media, social identity, and prejudice, with a keen focus on Catholicism and Islam as prime examples. Through a scrutiny of how these facets contribute to biased mindsets, we gain insights into the underlying mechanisms fueling prevalent biases in our society.
