Introduction
The crucial stabilizing structure within the knee joint is the anterior cruciate ligament (ACL), commonly subject to injuries arising from athletic pursuits or unforeseen accidents. To restore knee stability and function, a frequently recommended surgical intervention is anterior cruciate ligament reconstruction (ACLR). Among the array of graft options available, the bone-patellar tendon-bone (BPTB) autograft has emerged as a well-established and frequently employed technique. This essay delves into the utilization of BPTB autografts in ACLR, scrutinizing their merits, demerits, potential complications, and preventive strategies within the procedure.
