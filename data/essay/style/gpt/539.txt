Introduction:
The tourism industry in the Caribbean, like many others worldwide, has been profoundly affected by the global COVID-19 pandemic. Given the region's heavy reliance on tourism as a driver of economic progress, the challenges faced have been substantial. Nevertheless, by employing robust planning, fostering collaborations, and embracing innovation, the Caribbean tourism sector has managed to rejuvenate itself post-pandemic. This piece will delve into the elements that have underpinned the successful recovery of the region and the tactics currently in play to entice a greater influx of visitors.
