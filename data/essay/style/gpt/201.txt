Abstract:
The realm of management accounting has undergone a profound shift with the advent of digitalization, necessitating a deeper understanding of its effects on the profession and the skill sets imperative for tackling forthcoming challenges. This paper scrutinizes the research design and methodology implemented in a study that delves into the digitalization of the management accounting sector. It elucidates on how the experimental research design aids in enriching our comprehension of the repercussions of digitalization on the profession, as well as the competencies essential for navigating impending challenges. Moreover, it delves into the data collection techniques utilized, encompassing both primary and secondary sources, and sheds light on the array of businesses and participants partaking in the study.
