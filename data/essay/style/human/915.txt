I, Tituba is about a black slave woman called Tituba who had many problems and was not treated fairly. The story talks about how she suffered because she was black and a woman. Condé, the writer, wanted to show Tituba’s side of the story because he thought it was important to tell the truth about how she was treated badly because of her skin color and being a woman. Tituba’s husband, John Indian, who was also a slave, didn’t treat her well even though he had his own faults. The book shows how men being in charge and racism affected people of color and especially women. Tituba faced many challenges in her life because she was a woman and black. Men had a lot of power over her, and the story shows how this affected her life. Tituba had to deal with many bad things, and the book talks about how she tried her best to cope with them.
