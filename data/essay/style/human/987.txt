Primary education for children in families who speak two languages can be hard for parents. They sometimes have trouble with different cultures, not understanding certain normal rules and traditions. For example, in the United States, it's common for parents to go to school, but in South Korea, that's seen as rude. Parents can feel uneasy when talking to teachers because of language problems. So, families getting involved is the most popular way to bring teachers and parents closer and talk better. This text wants to talk about how families and other teaching ways help kids who speak two languages.
