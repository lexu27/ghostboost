Moral universalism and moral relativism are two contrasting theories that try to understand the nature of morality. While moral relativism indicates that moral principles are subjective and different cultures and individuals vary, moral universalism argues that there are objective moral truthsthat apply to all people regardless of their cultural or individual perspectives. In terms of view, moral universalism provides a truthful understanding of morality, since the arguments in favor of moral relativism ultimately return to the existence of a common core code and the importance of regulating human relationships.
One of the most important arguments in favor of moral relativism is the cultural diversity.
