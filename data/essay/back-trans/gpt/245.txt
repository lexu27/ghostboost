Public corruption is a complex and multifaceted topic that affects individuals, organizations and societies as a whole, undermining public trust, undermining the rule of law and hinders the economic development. The explanations for public corruption can be divided into individual, organizational and social levels.This essay will examine these explanations using evidence of scientific research and examples in real life and at the same time analyze the effects of ethics on the criminal justice in combating and preventing public corruption.
At an individual level, some explanations for public corruption revolve around personal greed and the desire for personal profit. At the organizational level, public corruption can be attributed to systemic deficiencies within the state bodies. Weak institutional framework conditions, lack of transparency and inadequate mechanisms of accountability create an environment that promotes corruption.Usually weaker governance systems, less efficient public administration and a higher incidence of bribery.
For example, the notorious case "Insy's therapeutics" in the United States illustrates how organizational factors can contribute to public corruption. Insys therapeutics, a pharmaceutical company, has carried out corrupt practices to increase the sales of their opioid medication.To prescribe their medication through bribery and setbacks, which led to a widespread addiction and thousands of deaths. The organizational culture at Insy's therapeutics promoted corruption, since the maximization of profits became a higher goal and neglects ethical considerations and public security.
At the social level, corruption can be influenced by cultural and social norms that tolerate or even celebrate corrupt practices. In some societies, corruption is deeply rooted and becomes an accepted part of the social structure. This normalization of corruption increases the probability of people whoAppealed to corrupt actions. The case of the Petrobras scandal in Brazil illustrates the social effects of corruption. The scandal comprised millions of dollars of setbacks and bribes for politicians and managers in exchange for business admission. The culture of corruption in Brazil enabled this illegal networkto hinder the country's social and economic development.
