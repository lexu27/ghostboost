Diabetes is a chronic illness that affects millions of people worldwide. There are two main types of diabetes: Type 1 and type 2. Each type has its own mechanisms and symptoms as well as specific nutritional and medication schemes for treatment.
Type 1 diabetes is an autoimmune disease that occurs when the immune system incorrectly attacks the insulin-producing cells in the pancreas. Type -2 -Diabetes, on the other hand, is a metabolic disorder that occurs when the body becomes resistant to the effects of insulin or does not produce enough insulin to maintain normal blood sugar levels. Genetic predisposition, obesity, physical inactivity and poor nutrition are some of the factors.The symptoms of type -2 diabetes often develop gradually and can urinate frequent urination, increased thirst, blurred vision, numbness or tingling in the extremities and slow wound healing.
Management of type -2 diabetes is varied and includes changes in lifestyle, oral medication and in some cases insulin therapy. Loss of weight, regular physical activity and healthy diet are key components for the type -2 -diabetes. In summary, type 1 and type 2 diabetes differ in their core mechanisms, symptoms and management.Healthy nutrition and regular physical activity are essential for the treatment of both types of diabetes, but the specific recommendations can vary. With appropriate management, people with diabetes can live life and at the same time minimize the risk of complications in connection with the disease.
