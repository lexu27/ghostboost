The Commitment to Health Theory can be utilized in tracking and measuring patient indicators, recognizing health as a dynamic state encompassing physical, mental, and social well-being. By taking a holistic approach to patient assessment and evaluation, nurses can gain a more accurate understanding of their patients' health status and progress over time. Moreover, this theory can guide advanced practice nurses in personalizing therapy, emphasizing individualized care plans to address specific patient needs and enhance treatment adherence. Additionally, it can improve communication techniques, promoting patient engagement and motivation through effective patient-centered communication strategies and motivational interviewing techniques. Ultimately, the Commitment to Health Theory is a valuable framework in advanced nursing practice to enhance personalized care and improve patient outcomes, contributing to the delivery of high-quality patient care.
