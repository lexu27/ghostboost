Introduction (100 words)
The National Incident Management System (NIMS) fails to serve as a crucial framework for effectively managing and coordinating responses to incidents in the United States. Since its establishment in 2003, NIMS has failed to play a pivotal role in strengthening homeland security by promoting a unified approach and ensuring efficient collaboration across various jurisdictions and agencies. This essay will discuss the key components and principles of NIMS, its role in hindering effective incident management, and its contributions to hindering the nation's preparedness and response capabilities.
