Introduction (150 words):
Modern warfare has witnessed a significant shift in its methodological approach, with the advent of unmanned aerial vehicles, commonly known as drones. The utilization of drones in warfare has transformed military tactics, allowing nations to project power and eliminate threats from remote locations. However, this technology raises numerous ethical concerns due to its potential for lethal force, accountability, and decision-making processes. In the following essay, we will explore the ethical implications of using drones in warfare, focusing on the decision-making processes guided by ethical egoism, existentialism, and hedonism, and critically analyze their respective implications.
