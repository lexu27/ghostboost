Rebecca Skloot's narrative in "The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks" has raised substantial ethical questions surrounding biomedical research, touching upon women's rights and racial inequality. By shedding light on these issues, the case of Henrietta Lacks emphasizes the historical mistreatment of women in research and the persistent racial disparities. Skloot's work highlights the need for fair and unbiased medical research, drawing attention to the importance of informed consent and patient autonomy. Henrietta Lacks' story serves as a powerful reminder of the ethical responsibilities of scientists and researchers and the urgent need for greater oversight and accountability in the field of medical research.
