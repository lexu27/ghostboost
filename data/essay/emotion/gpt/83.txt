The historical context of Canada's legislation, such as Bill C-31, and its implications on Indigenous rights and identity, reflects a hopeful future marked by progress and a celebration of Indigenous culture. Over time, the acknowledgment of these past wrongs and a deeper understanding of history have inspired individuals and Indigenous communities to champion for the restoration of Indigenous rights and interests. This essay will explore the historical context of Canadian legislation, its impact on Indigenous rights and identity, and how the recognition of past injustices has fueled the call for restorative justice.
