Introduction (Approximately 150 words)
Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems have transformed healthcare by converting traditional paper-based medical records into digitized and easily accessible formats. In recent years, the spotlight has been on integrating EHR systems into nursing practice due to their potential benefits in enhancing patient care, streamlining workflows, improving interprofessional collaboration, and boosting patient satisfaction. This essay delves into the significance of incorporating EHR systems into nursing practice and puts forth a policy for utilizing EHR in drug administration, medication monitoring, treatment adherence, and substance abuse screening. Furthermore, this essay examines the obstacles and restrictions of current EHR systems and emphasizes the necessity for optimization to enhance efficient workflows, interprofessional care, and patient satisfaction.
