To prevent heart disease, cancer, and diabetes, people are advised to follow strict dietary guidelines. The American Heart Association emphasizes the importance of avoiding added sugars, high sodium levels, and unhealthy fats in their diet. They recommend incorporating whole grains, vegetables, nuts, seeds, and lean meats like chicken and beef. Similarly, the American Cancer Society suggests increasing intake of fruits and vegetables, particularly dark green and orange varieties, to reduce cancer risks. They also recommend high-fiber foods while limiting red meat and sugars. The American Diabetes Association advocates for a diet rich in non-starchy vegetables like broccoli and carrots, making up half of each meal, and emphasizes the benefits of proteins and healthy fats. Overall, the guidelines from these organizations stress the importance of avoiding harmful ingredients and increasing consumption of nutritious foods to prevent these health conditions.
