Afghanistan's geographical location has historically connected it to neighboring countries. The country's terrain is diverse, with mountainous regions dominating the landscape. The climate is subtropical continental, influenced by monsoon winds, leading to hot summers and generally mild winters. The flora is adapted to arid conditions, with desert landscapes interspersed with mountainous terrain. Afghanistan's rich history dates back thousands of years, resulting in a diverse population with various ethnicities and languages. The predominant religion is Sunni Islam, shaping societal norms and roles. Political life in Afghanistan has been marked by interventions, conflicts, and the presence of extremist groups like the Taliban, ISIS, al-Qaeda, and the Northern Resistance. The recent Taliban rule has reversed many reforms and advancements in women's rights and civil liberties achieved in the previous decade.
