The 1999 movie Girl, Interrupted portrayed life in a psychiatric hospital through the eyes of its patients. The main character, Susanna Kaysen, was sent to the institution after attempting suicide and being diagnosed with borderline personality disorder. Despite her talents in writing, painting, and music, the movie depicted her struggles with emotional issues and low self-esteem. Susanna's appearance and interactions with others hinted at her underlying sadness and anxiety. The use of lighting, music, and editing techniques highlighted the protagonist's inner turmoil and journey towards self-acceptance and recovery.
